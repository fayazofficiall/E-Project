Christ the Redeemer

An iconic statue symbolizing peace and faith

WONDERS WORLD

The Symbol of Peace

Christ the Redeemer is an iconic statue of Jesus Christ located atop the Corcovado Mountain in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. Completed in 1931, it stands as a symbol of Christianity and a beacon of peace and unity for people around the world.

One of the New Seven Wonders of the World, Christ the Redeemer is a masterpiece of art and engineering. The statue, with its outstretched arms, welcomes visitors from across the globe, offering a breathtaking view of Rio de Janeiro. It was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2012 as part of the "Carioca Landscapes Between the Mountain and the Sea."

Quick Facts
  • Location: Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
  • Built: 1922-1931
  • Architect: Paul Landowski & Heitor da Silva Costa
  • Height: 30 meters (98 feet), with a 8-meter (26 feet) pedestal
  • Materials: Reinforced concrete, soapstone
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site: 2012

History of Christ the Redeemer

1921

A proposal to build a large statue of Christ is made to mark the centenary of Brazil's independence.

1922

The foundation stone is laid on Mount Corcovado, and fundraising for the project begins.

1926

Construction officially begins under the direction of engineer Heitor da Silva Costa and sculptor Paul Landowski.

1931

The statue is completed and inaugurated on October 12, standing 30 meters tall with outstretched arms spanning 28 meters.

1980

Pope John Paul II visits Christ the Redeemer, reinforcing its significance as a global religious monument.

2006

A chapel is built at the base of the statue to mark its 75th anniversary, allowing visitors to hold weddings and baptisms.

2007

Christ the Redeemer is voted as one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.

2010

The statue undergoes a major restoration to repair damage caused by time and weather.

Interesting Facts

Majestic Location

Christ the Redeemer stands atop Mount Corcovado, offering breathtaking panoramic views of Rio de Janeiro.

Built with Soapstone

The statue is covered with thousands of triangular soapstone tiles, chosen for their durability and smooth texture.

Lighting Up the Sky

Christ the Redeemer is illuminated at night, making it a stunning sight visible from many parts of the city.

Massive Construction

The statue weighs approximately 635 metric tons and stands 30 meters tall with an arm span of 28 meters.

Religious Symbol

It is one of the most recognized religious monuments in the world and a symbol of Christianity and peace.

Restoration Efforts

Over the years, Christ the Redeemer has undergone several restoration projects to maintain its grandeur.

Architectural Marvel

Christ the Redeemer is an Art Deco statue made of reinforced concrete and covered with soapstone, designed to withstand the harsh weather conditions of Rio de Janeiro.

The statue’s arms stretch 28 meters wide, symbolizing peace and embracing the people of Brazil and the world.

It stands on a 26-foot pedestal, which houses a chapel where visitors can hold small religious ceremonies.

Location

Christ the Redeemer is located in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, atop Mount Corcovado in the Tijuca National Park.
