Taj Mahal

The crown jewel of India and a symbol of eternal love

WONDERS WORLD

The Monument of Love

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the southern bank of the Yamuna river in the Indian city of Agra. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal.

Considered the jewel of Muslim art in India, the Taj Mahal is one of the world's most celebrated structures and a symbol of India's rich history. The Taj Mahal was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983 for being "the jewel of Muslim art in India and one of the universally admired masterpieces of the world's heritage".

Quick Facts
  • Location: Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India
  • Built: 1632-1653
  • Architect: Ustad Ahmad Lahauri
  • Height: 73 meters (240 feet)
  • Materials: White marble, precious stones
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site: 1983

History of the Taj Mahal

1631

Mumtaz Mahal, Shah Jahan's beloved wife, dies during childbirth. The grief-stricken emperor vows to build a monument in her memory.

1632

Construction of the Taj Mahal begins. Ustad Ahmad Lahauri is appointed as the main architect.

1632-1639

Main mausoleum structure is built. Over 20,000 workers and 1,000 elephants were employed in the construction.

1643

The main dome and minarets are completed.

1648

The gardens and outlying buildings are completed.

1653

The Taj Mahal complex is finally completed after 22 years of construction at a cost of approximately 32 million rupees.

1658

Shah Jahan is deposed by his son Aurangzeb and imprisoned in Agra Fort, where he spends his final years gazing at the Taj Mahal across the river.

1666

After Shah Jahan's death, he is buried next to his wife in the Taj Mahal.

Interesting Facts

Precious Inlays

The white marble is inlaid with 28 types of precious and semi-precious stones brought from Tibet, China, Sri Lanka, and parts of India.

Perfect Symmetry

The Taj Mahal is perfectly symmetrical on all sides, with identical features mirrored on either side of the main dome.

Changing Colors

The Taj Mahal appears to change color throughout the day - pinkish in the morning, white in the evening, and golden in moonlight.

Massive Workforce

Over 20,000 workers and 1,000 elephants were used to build the monument. Artisans were brought from all over Asia.

Poetry in Marble

The walls of the Taj are inscribed with verses from the Quran, using a special form of calligraphy that appears uniform regardless of viewing distance.

Craftsmen's Hands

Legend says that Emperor Shah Jahan ordered the hands of the chief craftsmen to be cut off so that they would never be able to build another monument as beautiful as the Taj Mahal.

Architectural Marvel

The Taj Mahal represents the finest example of Mughal architecture, a style that combines elements from Persian, Ottoman Turkish and Indian architectural styles.

The main structure is made of semi-translucent white marble, carved with flowers and inlaid with thousands of semi-precious stones in beautiful patterns. The central dome is 58 feet in diameter and 213 feet high.

Four minarets frame the tomb, each 138 feet tall. The entire complex includes a mosque, a guest house, and is set in formal gardens bounded on three sides by a crenellated wall.

Location

The Taj Mahal is located in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India, on the southern bank of the Yamuna River.
